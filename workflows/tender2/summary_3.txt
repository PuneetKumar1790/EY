### 8. Evaluation Criteria

This section summarizes how the tender will be evaluated. The Technical Bid Evaluation Basis clarifies the criteria for technical acceptance. The Financial Bid Evaluation Basis explains how the lowest bidder (L1) will be determined. Preference Clauses highlight any special considerations (e.g., for MSMEs or local suppliers), and Weightage or Scoring Mechanism details any quantitative evaluation methods.

**Technical Bid Evaluation Basis:** Bids are submitted in a two-bid system: Techno-commercial Bid and Price Bid. The technical scrutiny committee of PGVCL evaluates the techno-commercial view. PGVCL reserves the right to open or not to open the price bid based on past performance of supplied materials. Price bids of only techno-commercially qualified bidders will be opened.
**Financial Bid Evaluation Basis:** Tenders will be evaluated on a "firm Price End Cost with GST and Cess as applicable basis," unless Total Owning Cost (TOC) is specified. L-1 will be determined based on this end cost. Negotiation and price matching may occur with L-1 New and lowest Regular Categories if there is a lack of serious competition.
**Preference Clauses:**
*   **Gujarat-based Parties:** Not less than 50% of the quantity may be reserved for Gujarat-based manufacturing units if they match the L-1 price and their quoted price is not more than 15% higher than L-1.
*   **MSME Preference (Gujarat):** Micro, Cottage, and Small Scale Industrial Units of Gujarat state may receive quantity allocation if they quote up to 10% higher than other units. Women-owned units within this category may be considered if they quote up to 11% higher. A quality-based price preference of up to 5% higher is applicable for ISI/BIS/Agmark certified materials if not mandatory by law. The total price preference cannot exceed 15%.
**Weightage or Scoring Mechanism:** L-1 regular parties will receive heavy weightage in order placement. Quantity distribution to New-1 Suppliers is restricted to 10% (30% for non-critical items), and New-2 Suppliers to 25% (40% for non-critical items), provided they match the L-1 price of the New Supplier category. For Cables, New Suppliers (New-1/New-2) are allotted up to 10%/25% of the tender quantity or minimum drum length, whichever is higher.
